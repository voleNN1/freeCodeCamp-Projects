Life of Ronald Reagan

From successful Actor to Beloved President

  • 1911: Born in Illinois to Nelle Wilson and Jack Reagan
  • 1928: Began attending Eureka College
  • 1932: Graduated Eureka with a Bachelor of Arts degree, at the same time taking a job in Iowa as a Sports Broadcaster
  • 1937: Moved to California to begin a contract with Warner and also joined the US Army Reserve
  • 1942: Drafted into Active Duty Serivce but with severe Near-sightedness
  • 1945: Discharged from the military after having created more than 400 Training Films
  • 1947: Elected to President of the Screen Actors Guild
  • 1952: Resigned as President of SAG but remained as a board member
  • 1959: Re-elected to President of SAG to help them gain residual payments from producers (Which he did sucessfully)
  • 1960: Resigned as President of SAG for the final time as well as left the board
  • 1967: Becomes Governor of California in a landslide victory
  • 1975: After eight successful years Reagan declines to run for office again ending his tenure as Governor
  • 1981: Elected as President of the United States in another landslide victory. This time against Jimmy Carter
  • 1989: Fulfulling two full terms and an attempted assassination Reagan will exit the office of the president and subsuquently the limelight only doing Speaking Engagements
  • 1991: Wrote a New York Times article in support of the "Brady Bill" or a Federal Assault Weapons Ban
  • 1994: After having been diagnosed with Alzheimer's Disease he retreats from the public eye fully and spends most of his time at his home or ranch
  • 2003: After a lengthy battle with his disease and complications due to Pneumonia Ronald W. Reagan passes away. Leaving a lasting legacy both domestically and globally
